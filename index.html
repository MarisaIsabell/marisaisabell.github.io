About

I am a computational biologist and postdoctoral researcher at the Novo Nordisk Foundation Center for Basic Metabolic Research, University of Copenhagen.

My work focuses on human-microbiome symbiosis, combining metagenomics, multi-omics, and computational biology to study how microbial communities interact with the human host in health and disease.

Research

I study host-associated microbial communities using metagenomics, proteogenomics, and multi-omics approaches.

My current research investigates proteogenomic and microbiome patterns in Greenlandic Inuit, with a broader interest in microbial ecology, biomarker discovery, and host-microbiome interactions.
