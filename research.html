Overview

My research focuses on human-microbiome symbiosis and the computational analysis of host-associated microbial communities. I use metagenomics, proteogenomics, and multi-omics approaches to study how microbiomes interact with the human host in health and disease.

Current project: Proteogenomics & microbiome in Greenlandic Inuit

My current postdoctoral work investigates proteogenomic and microbiome patterns in Greenlandic Inuit, with a focus on host-microbiome interactions and cardiometabolic health.

Human gut microbiome, enterotypes, and dysbiosis

During my doctoral research, I studied global fecal metagenomes to refine enterotype concepts and investigate microbial dysbiosis across human cohorts.

Liver disease and the gut microbiome

I have worked on microbiome-derived biomarkers in the context of liver disease, including alcohol-related liver disease and bile acid metabolism.

Cystic fibrosis lung microbiome

My earlier research investigated longitudinal lung microbiome dynamics in adolescents with cystic fibrosis.
